Day 06 · Saturday

Rest &
Recovery

🛌 Active Recovery
🌿

You Grow On Rest Days

Muscle is built during recovery, not during training. Protect this day. If you feel good, do light active recovery — not an extra workout.

Light options (optional): 20-min walk · Swimming · Yoga / mobility · Foam rolling · Light stretching

What to avoid: Heavy cardio · Extra lifting · Poor sleep

🧘 Mobility Work (Optional)
  • Hip flexor stretch — 2 × 60 sec
  • Lat + chest doorway stretch
  • Shoulder internal rotation work
  • Hamstring + calf stretch
  • Thoracic spine foam roll
😴 Recovery Checklist
  • Sleep 8–9 hours tonight
  • Eat at or slightly above maintenance
  • Drink 3–4L water
  • No alcohol — kills testosterone + recovery
  • Prep meals for next week

Progression Guide
  1. 01
    The 2-Rep Rule (Your Weekly Target)
    When you can hit the top of your rep range on all sets (e.g. all 4 sets of 6 reps on bench), add 2.5 kg next session. Never add weight if form breaks. This is your #1 rule.
  2. 02
    Track Every Single Session
    Log your weights and reps every day — phone notes, a small notebook, anything. You cannot progress what you don't measure. Without tracking, you will plateau within 4 weeks.
  3. 03
    Compound Lifts Are Priority
    Get stronger on Bench, OHP, Pull-Ups, Row, and Squat. Everything else follows. Accessory weights can stay the same for weeks — compound strength is what builds mass.
  4. 04
    Deload Every 4–6 Weeks
    Drop all weights by 40–50% for one full week. Same exercises, same reps, very light load. Your joints and nervous system need this. You come back stronger — every time.
  5. 05
    Commit for at Least 12 Weeks
    Don't change the program every few weeks. Newbie gains are the best gains of your life. Run this program for 12 weeks before even thinking about changing anything.
  6. 06
    Sleep is Your #1 Supplement
    You build muscle while sleeping — not while training. Get 8–9 hours every night. No supplement will ever replace sleep. Poor sleep = poor recovery = no gains, no matter how hard you train.
